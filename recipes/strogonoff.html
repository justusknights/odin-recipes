Strogonoff

Or

How to do something clever when I think it up

You're probably not used to seeing Strogonoff as I'm going to present it here but trust me, you'll appreciate it. This is the traditional Brazilian approach to the dish, so it will be served over rice and made with cubed beef roast instead of ground beef. Let's get into.

Ingredients

  • Any cut of beef, cut into short strips or small cubes, about 1lb
  • 2 cups of sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbspns olive oil
  • 3 small potatoes, cubed about the size of a fingernail
  • 1 quart of pure tomato sauce, no seasoning added
  • heavy cream, to desired consistency/color
  • 3 bay leaves
  • salt, pepper, and paprika to taste
  • Worcestershire, a few dashes
  • Potato straws/string potatoes as a garnish

Start by cooking your onion and garlic in olive oil on medium-high heat until the onion goes transparent. You'll then want to add your potatoes, beef, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaves, some salt and pepper and paprika, though you'll likely be adding more of those later. Let this cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally until the beef is cooked through and softening and the potatoes are soft.

At this point, you'll add your tomato sauce and mushrooms and let them cook all the way down on medium-low heat. You should end up with a reddish brown sauce that is relatively thick and smells delicious.

You're nearly done! Taste this concoction, it should be salty, a bit sweet, but still sharper and more acidic than you'd want for a main course, which is perfect since the cream will dampen those factors considerably. Add any other seasoning at this point, you can add a bit of crushed red pepper for a kick, more paprika, or anything else you enjoy, but get all of that done now, before adding the cream.

When you are happy with your base drop the heat to low and add the cream slowly. There is no "right" amount of cream, but there is definitely a wrong amount of cream. So go slow, you're looking for a pinkish brown, slightly thick and creamy sauce. If you're paying attention, you'll know when you're there. Do not let the sauce cook very long once the cream is added or the cream will begin to seperate; this does not harm the flavor, but will make the dish look less appetizing.

Once the cream is fully combined and the dish warm, serve over a bed of rice and top with potato straws. You now have made Brazilian Strogonoff. THe meat can be substituted for chicken or shrimp, and the mushrooms and potatoes are optional. Play around and see what you like best, but whatever the case, make sure you're sharing with people that matter to you.

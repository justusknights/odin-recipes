Focaccia Bread

Or

How to make friends and influence people

In case you've not been around for the past few years and missed it: Everyone is bananas for Focaccia bread now. They can't get enough of the stuff. Consider this recipe your first class ticket to jump on the band wagon.

Before working yourself into a tizzy thinking bread is complex and takes very precise measurements and temperatures...Don't believe everything you hear or read or are told by experts, this bread is nearly idiot-proof

...So you're in luck.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of bread flour
  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 1.5 teaspoons of instant yeast
  • 2 teaspoons sea salt, plus more as a topping
  • .25 cups of Extra Virgin Olive Oil plus some extra

Now lets get cracking

You're going to want to put the flour, salt, and yeast into a large bowl and whisk or toss it together.

Add your water and olive oil and mix everything together. If you want to feel traditional and italian, use a wooden spoon, although a mixer with a dough hook is much easier. You'll find the dough gets gummy rather quickly, and you'll want resort to hand kneading, still in the bowl, no need to dirty a counter-top.

Once the dough is consistently gummy, form into a loose ball, cover with plastic wrap and leave someplace warm-ish (in the 70's-80's F) until it gets bigger...maybe double in size, maybe a little more, it's not that big of a deal, 2 hours should do.

While the dough is rising, although hopefully this doesn't take you the full 2 hours, oil a pan, with some of the extra olive oil mentioned above. A cast iron skillet, a 9x13 or even just a flat cookie sheet will do, so long as it is well oiled.

When the dough is double in size, more or less, remove it from the mixing bowl and drop it onto the oiled surface. Spread the dough gently towards the edges of the surface until it is a consistent thickness. Spread some more oil over the top and then cover with plastic wrap. At this point you have several options. You can cook the bread now, or you can let it rest in a fridge for up to three days. If you let it rest, just make sure it is mostly airtight so it does not dry out. The longer it rests, the better.

Whatever you decide, you'll need to cook it at some point and when you do, you'll want the oven on 450F and you'll cook your foccacia for around 25 minutes, so be ready to preheat the oven while you do the next step.

While the oven heats, take the plastic off the bread and drizzle more oil on top. Use your fingers to gently poke into the top of the bread, creating a bunch of uniform little craters across the surface. This will temporarily deflate your dough, but don't worry. Sprinkle some course ground sea salt on top for flavor. Leave the dough on the counter to warm to room temperature, it will cook better if it doesn't go into the oven cold. This will also give it time to rise a bit more.

When the dough is no longer cold ot the touch, place it in the oven and set a timer for 20 minutes. All ovens heat differently, so start with 20 minutes and check it. After that, just watch it until it looks golden brown like the photo above.

Remove it from the oven when ready, place it on a cooling rack and let it cool enough to hold it comfortably, but definitely try it while it is still warm!

You've made your first Focaccia Bread, wasn't that easy!? Now you can customize your next one. Feel free to add herbs to the dough or on top, or you can add garlic and olives before you bake, or anything else you might like. But what really mattters is how much more popular you will find yourself when other people realize you can make focaccia bread. You'll suddenly find your self flooded with invitations to pot-lucks and cheese tastings, so brush up on your snooty upper-middle class vocabulary and start learning about trust funds because the bourgeoisie will be adopting you before you know it.
